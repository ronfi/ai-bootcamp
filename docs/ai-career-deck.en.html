01 · New Rules · Both Sides of the Table Have Changed

Your résumé's first reader may not be human

The recruiter's AI

61% of employers screen résumés with AI, and 96% of recruiting professionals use AI in at least one step; AI-led first-round interviews rose from 10% to 34% in two years — more and more, the one scoring you across the keyboard is an algorithm.

The candidate's AI

81% of job-seekers already use or plan to use AI in their search; the most common use isn't "ghostwriting" but ATS compatibility checks (64%). Job-seekers who don't use AI are already the minority.

Two corollaries: ① Your résumé must first pass the machine gate — keywords, structure, parseability; artsy layouts cost you points. ② When 81% of candidates use AI, "using AI" is no longer an edge — "using it better than the rest" is — and that's exactly what today teaches.

02

The Method: an AI Job-Search Workflow

Remember Session 1's "advanced workflow" — knowledge base, brief, iterate, review, ship? Your job search is your first real project. Run the exact same playbook.

02 · The Method · Step 1

Knowledge base: make the target industry your home turf

> Here are 100 JDs for "sell-side equity research analyst" I collected (attached).
Help me: 1) extract the 15 most frequent requirements, ranked by frequency;
2) split them into "hard gates" (certificates/degrees) vs "fast-learnable skills";
3) write the output to knowledge-base/jd-analysis.md
✓ Top 15 high-frequency requirements generated:
  #1 Financial modeling (mentioned in 87/100)
  #2 Industry research frameworks (79/100)
  #3 Python / data processing (61/100, up sharply from last year)…

Mine 100 JDs

Don't guess what the job wants — let AI count it. The high-frequency requirements are your study syllabus, and year-over-year comparison even shows where the industry is heading.

Build target-company dossiers

One document per target company: business lines, latest earnings highlights, executives' public remarks, campus-hiring process and past questions — your ammo depot for the night before the interview.

Let the intel update itself

cron + Agent watches your target companies daily (news, filings, recruiting accounts); every change flows into the knowledge base automatically — Session 2's scheduled tasks, working for you for the first time.

02 · The Method · Step 2

Positioning brief: one page on "who I am, where I'm going"

What I have

Courses, projects, internships, skills, certificates — dump it all on the AI and have it re-sort everything in the recruiter's language: which are assets, which are merely experiences.

What I want

Industry × role × city × bottom lines (pay/overtime/growth). Only once written down do you notice how many "wants" fight each other — make the trade-offs before applying, not at the offer stage.

Gap list

Match "what I have" against the high-frequency JD requirements and have AI produce a gap list plus a catch-up plan ranked by ROI: what can be closed in two weeks (tools, question banks), and what not to chase (gatekeeping certificates).

This is the job-search version of the "goals & constraints spec": feed it first into every later session (résumé edits, mock interviews, cover letters) — the deeper AI knows you, the sharper its help.
02 · The Method · Step 3

Résumé engineering: one résumé per job, robots love it too

> Rewrite my résumé against this JD (résumé and positioning brief are in the project).
Rules: 1) annotate the reason for every change; 2) quantify every result;
3) use the JD's own wording without keyword stuffing; 4) never invent any experience.
✓ 11 changes made. Example:
  Before: "Helped run the student club's social media account"
  After: "Ran the account solo for 8 months, published 32 posts,
        top post 12K reads, followers +40%"
  Reason: JD item 3 asks for "content production skills"; quantified = verifiable

One résumé per job

A generic résumé is an average-score résumé. Have AI re-tune the emphasis and keywords for each JD — 10 minutes per version, a luxury only the AI era makes possible.

Quantify results (STAR)

"Participated / responsible for / assisted" are dead words. For every experience, force yourself to answer: how many, how fast, up by how much — AI is great at pressing, so make it your coach.

⚠️ Red line

Polishing ≠ fabricating: every number must survive a background check; never list what you didn't do. Every sentence AI writes, you must be able to defend through three levels of follow-up questions.

One table rules them all

Where you applied, current status, deadlines, next action — a spreadsheet, or let an Agent keep it; cron pings you every morning with today's deadlines.

Find the bottleneck with the funnel

Review conversion weekly: application→test low? Fix the résumé; test→interview low? Drill problems; interview→offer low? Practice interviews. The data tells you which stage to repair — no need for blind anxiety.

02 · The Method · Step 6

Mock interviews: an on-call interviewer who shows no mercy

> You are a stern senior interviewer at a brokerage research institute.
Dig into my résumé (in the project) section by section;
after every answer, press me with two follow-ups, and at the end give:
1) per-question feedback 2) my three verbal tics 3) an improvement list
Interviewer: you wrote "independently produced an industry report" —
  what were the data sources? How did you define the scope?
  If I say your valuation is 30% too high, how do you defend it?

Three interview types, drilled separately

Behavioral: STAR deep-dive follow-ups; technical: questions set from the JD; stress: have AI play the "hard-to-please" interviewer — you stay calm in the room because you've met the hard questions before.

Record → AI review

Record live mocks or self-Q&A, transcribe, hand it to AI: verbal tics, broken logic, pacing — Session 2's video-transcription exercise, put to work here.

The information-asymmetry weapon

The night before: have AI use the company knowledge base to write 10 "questions this company is most likely to ask me" — most of your rivals never take this step.

02 · The Method · Step 7

Negotiating the offer: don't walk the last mile unarmed

Check the market first

Have AI compile the role's salary band (platform data, peer disclosures, per-capita pay in annual reports), and negotiate with a range in hand instead of being anchored to HR's first number.

Rehearse the negotiation

Have AI play HR for three rounds: "our pay bands are standardized", "new grads have no room to negotiate" — a comeback for every script, and the cost of blowing up paid in rehearsal.

Multi-offer decision matrix

Pay, growth, platform, city, overtime — have AI build the scoring matrix, with weights you set yourself (back to the positioning brief). AI supplies structure; the human makes the choice.

The method in one line, seven steps: knowledge base to scout the field → positioning brief to lock the target → résumé to pass the machine gate → work to win the human gate → funnel to track progress → mock interviews to build nerve → data as armor when talking money. Now watch two majors run the full play.

04 · Case: Software · Weeks 5–6

Algorithms & system design: AI as sparring partner, not hired gun

The right way to grind problems

Stuck? Don't peek at the answer — have AI give hints, not solutions ("only hint at the data-structure direction"); once solved, have it generate variant problems to test whether you understood or memorized.

System design simulation

Have AI play interviewer: "design a timetable-reminder system", pressing step by step on concurrency, storage, degradation — Shuai's Agent project is ready-made material, turning recital into a conversation about his own architecture.

⚠️ One hard red line

No proxies in online assessments. Big-tech anti-cheat keeps upgrading, and one blacklist flag follows you far beyond a single company — use AI to raise your real level, not to fake one.

Asked in an interview "how would you solve this with AI?", the right answer isn't embarrassment — it's showing the workflow: "I'd have it enumerate the edge cases first, write the core logic myself, then have it review — exactly like every commit in my GitHub."
04 · Case: Software · Wrapping Up

"An engineer who directs AI" — how to write it without sounding hollow

Résumé wording (before / after)

❌ "Familiar with ChatGPT, Claude and other AI tools"
✅ "Independently delivered a Job-Funnel Agent via an AI-pairing + test-driven workflow (Python/FastAPI, 34 commits, 85% test coverage), plus 2 merged PRs to the open-source project Hermes" — tools are worthless; what you deliver with the tools is what's worth money.

Interview narrative (three sentences)

What I wrote (core logic and architecture decisions), what AI wrote (boilerplate and first-draft test cases), and how I made sure AI's code was right (review + tests + staged rollout) — those three sentences are the model answer to "can review and verify AI-generated code" in 2026 JDs.

The shared core of both cases: Mei and Shuai aren't selling "I can use AI" — they're selling "I built something with AI" — something verifiable, demonstrable, and able to survive cross-examination.

Red Lines & Mindset

AI adds leverage to your job search — and leverage amplifies both your equity and your losses. Three red lines to hold the floor, one mindset to hold the direction.

05 · Red Lines & Mindset · Do Not Cross

Three red lines — cross one, lose everything

① No résumé fraud

AI makes inventing stories easy — and makes background checks easy too. Numbers, titles, dates: one lookup and you're caught — and flagged candidates land on industry-shared blacklists.

② No proxies in tests or interviews

AI proxy-taking = cheating, and anti-cheat systems (camera behavior analysis, code fingerprinting) are upgrading with AI too. You'd be competing with AI at cheating. You can't win.

③ Full candor when asked

"How much of this work was AI?" — the model answer is to open the workflow and walk through it. Owning your usage is a plus; concealing it is an instant veto.

Interviewers can smell "AI flavor" now: ornate but hollow adjectives, projects with no details, collapse after two follow-ups. AI content you haven't digested scores worse than using no AI at all.
05 · Red Lines & Mindset · One Mindset
AI is an amplifier:
if you have work, it amplifies the work;
if all you have is blankness, it amplifies the blankness.
So the order cannot be reversed: first use AI to make something real (however small), then use AI to package and spread it. A portfolio is a compounding asset — Session 1 said "GitHub = the new résumé"; by now you should see it isn't the résumé's attachment. It is the résumé.
